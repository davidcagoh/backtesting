## Thesis

Two fixes to `TrendFilter200`:
1. **Window 200 → 720** (720h ≈ 30d). The 200h ≈ 8d window was too short to define a crypto regime — every bear bounce triggered a bull-trap entry.
2. **Slope gate** — require `sma720 > sma720.shift(24)` (SMA has risen over the prior 24h) before accepting an entry. This blocks entries when price pops above a still-declining SMA.

## Calmar metric note

Freqtrade reports two Calmar values:
- **Closed-trades Calmar** — computed on the return distribution of actual trades only. Correct for sparse strategies.
- **Daily wallet balance Calmar** — computed on day-by-day portfolio value including flat (cash) days. When most days have 0% change (no position), the mean daily return appears negative even if closed-trade profit is positive. This is the metric used in prior leaderboard entries.

Going forward the leaderboard should show **closed-trades Calmar** as primary. Prior entries should be re-run to get this metric for apples-to-apples comparison.

## What worked

- **Slope gate eliminated whipsaw.** 6 trades vs 49 (LongOnlyStrategy) and 90 (TrendFilter200). The slope gate correctly rejected most cross-up signals in the sustained bear.
- **MDD dramatically reduced:** 0.30% vs 1.86% (LongOnlyStrategy) and 4.22% (TrendFilter200).
- **Positive total return (+0.80%)** in a -30.76% market — first strategy to finish in profit.
- **Closed-trade Calmar 28.96** — best result on the leaderboard, though based on only 6 trades.

## What didn't

- **Thin sample.** 6 trades over 177 days; 1 winner (+10.98%) rescuing 5 losers (avg ~-1%). Closed-trade Calmar is statistically fragile at N=6.
- **Win rate still low (16.7%).** Long-only in a sustained bear: even well-filtered entries can't overcome the regime. The slope gate reduced entries but didn't improve entry quality enough.
- **Bull-market validation pending.** H7 in `learnings.md` requires positive Calmar in both regimes before live consideration. Not yet tested properly on a bull window.

## Interpretation

The slope gate works as a whipsaw suppressor. The 1h result is the best closed-trade Calmar seen so far, but the sample is too thin to be trusted on its own, and we don't yet have a bull-period validation. The strategy is **promising but not confirmed**.

The remaining open question (H5): does an HMM-based regime filter — which produces probabilistic state assignments rather than hard cross-up/break thresholds — improve both win rate and bull-window performance? The SMA regime filter family is not ruled out; it just needs either (a) proper bull-market validation, or (b) replacement with a probabilistic signal.
