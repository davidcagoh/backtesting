**Strategy file:** `user_data/strategies/TrendFilter200.py`
**Data:** BTC/USDC:USDC, 1h, 2025-10-06 → 2026-04-24 (5001 candles, ~200 days)
**Config:** `--eps --max-open-trades 1`, stoploss -10%, ROI disabled, exit on trend break (close < SMA200)

---

## Thesis

Crypto has persistent regimes at multi-day+ timeframes. A 200-period SMA regime filter should sit in cash through bear markets and only hold during up-trends. Over the 2025-10 → 2026-04 window the market fell 37% — a working trend filter should heavily outperform buy-and-hold and beat the SMA-cross placeholder on Calmar / MDD.

## What worked

- **MDD stayed bounded** at 4.22% while market fell 37% — the filter is doing its job on the downside in the sense that you never get deeply drawn down.
- **No catastrophic loss** — -3% total vs -37% market.

## What didn't

- **Thesis failed on this window.** Calmar and Sharpe are *worse* than the placeholder (-6.84 / -2.54 vs -4.55 / -0.36). MDD also worse (4.22% vs 1.86%).
- **12.2% win rate and 26 consecutive losses.** The SMA200 cross-up in a structural bear market is overwhelmingly a bull-trap signal. 79 losing trades out of 90.
- **Stoploss -10% is too loose for the signal's edge.** Most losers hit stop or nearly so; winners are capped at +5%. Negative asymmetry.

## Interpretation

A plain 200-period SMA regime filter does not work as-is on this data window. The mechanism failed because:

1. The test window is a single sustained bear — every "up-trend" signal is local, not a regime shift.
2. Cross-up on the 1h timeframe is too noisy; 200 periods ≈ 8 days, which is not long enough to define a regime in crypto.
3. Without a higher-timeframe trend agreement (e.g. daily SMA200), 1h signals get whipsawed.

## Next test

Not a pure dead end — the family ("regime filters on crypto majors") is still worth exploring, but not as currently specified. Possible next experiments:

- **Longer regime window.** 1h SMA 720 (~30d) or a daily-timeframe filter applied to 1h execution.
- **Require slope confirmation**, not just cross — `sma_200.diff(24) > 0` before entry.
- **Pair with a short leg or a volatility filter.** The false positives are concentrated in high-vol declining periods.

Worth recording in `wiki/learnings.md` as an **Open Hypothesis refinement** (not yet "Ruled Out" — a single-window bear result doesn't rule out the family), but the naive 1h SMA200 crossover specifically should go to Ruled Out with the mechanism: *short-window regime filter on a single-asset, single-timeframe signal gets whipsawed in sustained bears.*
