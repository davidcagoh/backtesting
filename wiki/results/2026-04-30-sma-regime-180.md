## What worked

- **Bull-window Calmar positive (8.68)** — cleared the H7 bar. Strategy captures uptrend exposure while sitting out most of the 2025-2026 bear.
- **MDD tightly bounded:** 1.74% over 802 days. Never deeply underwater.
- **Profit factor 2.72** — robust signal that when the strategy does win, it wins big. The +54.96% trade anchors this.
- **More trades than SmaRegime720 bear-only (32 vs 6)** — SQN = 1.02 vs ~0.4; still thin but more meaningful.

## What didn't

- **Absolute return lags buy-and-hold significantly** (+6.33% vs +61.43% market). Long-only trend-following in a mostly-bull window will always trail; the question is risk-adjusted performance, not absolute.
- **Win rate still low (21.9%).** 7 wins, 25 losses. The single +54.96% trade does heavy lifting.
- **Sharpe (CT) = 0.14** is weak. The win tail is fat (one very large win) but rare. A single event dominates returns.
- **SQN = 1.02** — "below average" per Van Tharp's scale (<1.6). Tradeable but not strong.
- **N=32 with zero fees.** Hyperliquid taker fee ~0.035% per side = ~0.07% round trip. Over 32 trades this is roughly 2.24% in fees — eating most of the 6.33% gross return. **Cost modeling is now critical.**

## Interpretation

The slope-gate SMA regime family passes the minimum H7 bar: it generates positive risk-adjusted returns in both bull and bear windows. The 4h/SMA180 variant is more robust than the 1h/SMA720 bear-only test (32 trades, SQN 1.02 vs 6 trades, SQN <0.5).

However, two significant caveats remain before live consideration:
1. **Transaction costs not modeled.** Estimated ~2.24% in taker fees would absorb most of the 6.33% gain. Must re-run with realistic fee config.
2. **Thin win sample dominance.** One +54.96% trade accounts for the bulk of profit. The strategy's viability depends on catching bull runs early; if entry timing degrades slightly (e.g., looser criteria), this outlier may disappear.
